1.  GETTING STARTED
================================================================================

LocationMapApp is an Android map application that displays points of interest,
live transit vehicles, aircraft, weather, webcams, radar, and geofence alerts
on an interactive map. The app uses OpenStreetMap data and connects to multiple
live data sources.

When you first open the app:
  - The map centers on your GPS location automatically.
  - Points of interest (POIs) load for your visible area.
  - Weather data fetches automatically and appears in the toolbar.
  - The status line at the top shows your GPS coordinates and weather summary.

Requirements:
  - Android device running Android 14 (API 34) or later.
  - GPS/Location services enabled.
  - Network connection to the cache proxy server.


================================================================================
2.  MAP NAVIGATION
================================================================================

PANNING
  Drag the map with one finger to scroll in any direction. As you pan, POIs
  for the visible area load automatically from the cache.

ZOOMING
  - Pinch to zoom in or out.
  - Use the zoom slider on the right edge of the screen (+ and - buttons).
  - Double-tap to zoom in.

LONG PRESS (Manual Mode)
  Press and hold on any point on the map for about 2 seconds to:
  - Center the map on that location.
  - Auto-zoom to level 14 if currently zoomed out further.
  - Search for POIs at that location.
  - Fetch fresh weather data and alerts for that area.
  This is useful for exploring areas away from your current GPS position.

ZOOM LEVEL BEHAVIOR
  Different layers appear at different zoom levels:
  - Zoom 10+: POIs and aircraft appear.
  - Zoom 12+: School zones, flood zones, and railroad crossings appear.
  - Zoom 15+: Bus stops appear.
  - Zoom 18+: POI markers switch from colored dots to labeled icons with
    the place name and type.


================================================================================
3.  TOOLBAR & STATUS LINE
================================================================================

The toolbar is the slim bar at the very top of the screen.

TOOLBAR ICONS (left to right):
  - Weather icon (left): Shows current conditions. Tap to open the full
    weather dialog. A red border appears when weather alerts are active.
  - Dark Mode toggle (moon icon): Tap to switch between light and dark
    map tiles.
  - Alerts icon: Shows the severity of active geofence alerts.
      Gray = no alerts
      Blue = informational
      Yellow = warning
      Red = critical
      Pulsing red = emergency
    Tap to open the Alerts menu.
  - Grid Menu button (grid icon, far right): Tap to open the main menu
    with all feature buttons.

STATUS LINE
  The thin line just below the toolbar shows context-sensitive information:
  - When idle: Your GPS coordinates and a brief weather summary.
  - When following a vehicle or aircraft: Follow status and info.
  - When scanning for POIs: Scan progress.
  - When a geofence alert is active: Alert details with a color-coded
    background matching the zone type.


================================================================================
4.  GRID MENU
================================================================================

Tap the grid icon (top-right) to open the main dropdown menu. It contains
12 buttons arranged in a 3x4 grid:

  Row 1:  Transit  |  Webcams  |  Aircraft  |  Radar
  Row 2:  POI      |  Utility  |  Find      |  Go To
  Row 3:  Social   |  Chat     |  Profile   |  Legend

TRANSIT    — Open the transit layer menu (buses, trains, subway).
WEBCAMS    — Toggle the webcam layer on or off.
AIRCRAFT   — Open the aircraft menu (toggle, refresh frequency, auto-follow).
RADAR      — Toggle the animated NEXRAD weather radar overlay.
POI        — Open the POI categories menu to toggle which types are searched.
UTILITY    — Access utilities: Populate POIs, 10km Probe, Legend.
FIND       — Open the Find dialog to search for POIs by category or name.
GO TO      — Open the Go to Location dialog to navigate to an address.
SOCIAL     — Register an account or view your profile.
CHAT       — Open the chat room list for real-time messaging.
PROFILE    — View your user profile (display name, role, user ID).
LEGEND     — View the map legend showing all POI categories and colors.

5.  POINTS OF INTEREST (POI)
================================================================================

The app displays 17 categories of POIs sourced from OpenStreetMap:

  1.  Food & Drink — restaurants, fast food, cafes, bars, bakeries, delis
  2.  Fuel & Charging — gas stations, EV charging stations
  3.  Transit — train stations, bus stations, bike rentals, ferry terminals
  4.  Civic & Gov — town halls, post offices, courthouses, libraries, police
  5.  Parks & Rec — parks, playgrounds, beaches, trails, sports fields, pools
  6.  Shopping — supermarkets, malls, clothing, hardware, convenience stores
  7.  Healthcare — hospitals, pharmacies, clinics, dentists, veterinarians
  8.  Education — schools, universities, colleges, kindergartens
  9.  Lodging — hotels, motels, hostels, guest houses, campgrounds
  10. Parking — parking lots and garages
  11. Finance — banks, ATMs, credit unions
  12. Places of Worship — churches, mosques, synagogues, temples
  13. Tourism & History — museums, monuments, attractions, viewpoints, zoos
  14. Emergency Services — fire stations, hospitals, emergency rooms
  15. Auto Services — car repair, car wash, car rental, car dealers
  16. Entertainment — cinemas, theaters, nightclubs, casinos, arcades
  17. Offices & Services — post boxes, laundry, dry cleaning, tailors

POI DISPLAY
  - At low zoom: POIs appear as small colored dots (each category has a
    unique color).
  - At zoom 18+: POIs display as labeled icons showing the type and name.
  - Only POIs within the visible area are shown (viewport-only display).

TAPPING A POI MARKER
  Tap any POI marker on the map to open the POI Detail Dialog showing:
  - Distance and direction from your position.
  - Type (category).
  - Address.
  - Phone number (if available).
  - Operating hours (if available).
  - Website (with a "Load Website" button if available).
  - Comments section (see Section 18).
  - Action buttons at the bottom:
      DIRECTIONS — Open turn-by-turn directions in Google Maps.
      CALL       — Dial the phone number (if available).
      REVIEWS    — Open Google reviews for this place.
      MAP        — Open in Google Maps.
      SHARE      — Share the POI details (name, address, phone, hours,
                   and a Google Maps link) via any sharing app.

FAVORITE A POI
  In the POI Detail Dialog, tap the star icon in the header to add or remove
  the POI from your favorites. A filled star means it is a favorite; an
  outline star means it is not. See Section 7 for more on Favorites.

6.  FIND & SEARCH
================================================================================

Open the Find dialog from the grid menu (FIND button).

TEXT SEARCH
  At the top of the Find dialog is a search bar. Type at least 2 characters
  to search POIs by name. Results appear with a colored category dot and
  the distance from the map center. Tap any result to open its detail dialog.

FAVORITES CELL
  The first cell in the category grid is a gold star labeled "Favorites"
  with a count badge. Tap it to see all your saved favorites sorted by
  distance.

CATEGORY GRID
  Below the search bar is a grid of all 17 POI categories, each showing
  the count of POIs within a 10-kilometer radius of the map center.
  - Tap a category to see its subtypes (e.g., Food & Drink breaks down
    into Restaurants, Fast Food, Cafes, etc.).
  - Tap a subtype to see a distance-sorted list of matching POIs.
  - Tap any result to open its detail dialog.

FILTER MODE (Long Press)
  Long-press a category cell to enter filter mode. The map will show ONLY
  POIs from that category. A banner appears at the top — tap the banner
  to exit filter mode and restore all POIs.


================================================================================
7.  FAVORITES
================================================================================

You can save any POI as a favorite for quick access later.

ADDING A FAVORITE
  Open any POI Detail Dialog (by tapping a marker or finding it via search)
  and tap the star icon in the header. The star fills in to confirm.

REMOVING A FAVORITE
  Tap the filled star icon again in the POI Detail Dialog. The star returns
  to an outline.

VIEWING FAVORITES
  Open the Find dialog and tap the "Favorites" cell (gold star, first in the
  grid). Your favorites appear sorted by distance from the current map center.

8.  WEATHER
================================================================================

TOOLBAR WEATHER ICON
  The weather icon on the left side of the toolbar shows the current
  conditions at your location. It updates automatically every 30 minutes
  and whenever you get a new GPS fix. A red border around the icon means
  there are active weather alerts in your area.

WEATHER DIALOG
  Tap the weather icon to open the full Weather Dialog with four sections:

  CURRENT CONDITIONS
    Temperature, feels-like, wind speed and direction, humidity, dewpoint,
    visibility, barometric pressure, and a description of sky conditions.

  HOURLY FORECAST (48 hours)
    Hour-by-hour breakdown showing temperature, precipitation chance, wind,
    and conditions for the next 2 days.

  7-DAY OUTLOOK
    Daily forecast with high/low temperatures, precipitation, and conditions.

  WEATHER ALERTS
    Active NWS alerts for your area (severe thunderstorm warnings, flood
    watches, etc.). Alerts are expandable — tap to read full details.

METAR MARKERS
  Aviation weather (METAR) stations appear on the map as markers showing
  temperature, wind, and sky conditions in a compact format. Tap a METAR
  marker for a human-readable weather summary from that station.

9.  MBTA TRANSIT (Buses, Trains, Subway)
================================================================================

The Transit layer shows live Massachusetts Bay Transportation Authority
(MBTA) vehicles on the map in real time.

ENABLING TRANSIT
  Open the grid menu and tap TRANSIT to access transit layer controls.

LIVE VEHICLES
  - Buses appear as directional arrow markers.
  - Commuter rail trains show with next-stop ETA badges.
  - Subway trains appear on their respective lines.
  - Stale vehicles (no update for 2+ minutes) are detected and flagged.

TRAIN STATIONS (~270 stations)
  Tap any train station marker to open its Arrival Board showing:
  - Real-time predictions for arriving trains.
  - Auto-refreshes every 30 seconds.
  - Tap a specific train to view its full Trip Schedule.

BUS STOPS (~7,900 stops)
  Bus stops appear at zoom level 15 and above. Tap any bus stop marker
  to see its Arrival Board with real-time bus predictions.

VEHICLE DETAIL DIALOG
  Tap any vehicle marker (bus, train, or subway) to see:
  - Route, headsign, speed, and current status.
  - Three action buttons:
      FOLLOW     — Track this vehicle on the map. The map pans to follow
                   it as it moves. Tap the banner at the top to stop.
      VIEW ROUTE — See the full route on the map.
      ARRIVALS   — See arrival predictions for this vehicle's next stops.

FOLLOWING A VEHICLE
  When following a vehicle, the map automatically pans to keep it centered.
  POIs along the route are prefetched. If the vehicle data becomes stale
  (no update for 2+ minutes), you are notified. Tap the status banner to
  stop following.

10. AIRCRAFT TRACKING
================================================================================

The Aircraft layer shows live aircraft positions from the OpenSky Network.

ENABLING AIRCRAFT
  Open the grid menu and tap AIRCRAFT to access aircraft controls:
  - Toggle aircraft layer on/off (default: off).
  - Set refresh frequency (30 seconds to 5 minutes).
  - Enable auto-follow mode.

AIRCRAFT DISPLAY
  - Aircraft appear at zoom level 10 and above.
  - Each aircraft shows as a rotated airplane icon pointing in its
    direction of travel.
  - Callsign is displayed next to the icon.
  - Icons are color-coded by altitude (lower = warmer colors,
    higher = cooler colors).
  - SPI emergency status is highlighted when active.

TAPPING AN AIRCRAFT
  Tap any aircraft marker to enter Follow Mode:
  - The map tracks the aircraft globally (even as it flies to new areas).
  - A flight path trail draws behind the aircraft, color-coded by altitude.
  - Flight info appears in the status banner.
  - The map pans automatically to keep the aircraft centered.
  - Tap the status banner to stop following.
  - If the aircraft disappears (lands or goes out of range), the app
    tolerates up to 3 missed updates before stopping.

AUTO-FOLLOW MODE
  When enabled in the Aircraft menu, the app automatically selects and
  follows aircraft:
  - Only follows aircraft above 10,000 feet.
  - Rotates to a new aircraft every 20 minutes.
  - Smart switching based on altitude, POI proximity, and US bounds.

13. GEOFENCE ALERTS
================================================================================

The Geofence Alert System warns you when you (or an aircraft you are
following) approaches or enters certain zones.

ACCESSING ALERTS
  Tap the Alerts icon in the toolbar (bell icon, right side) to open the
  Alerts menu.

FIVE ZONE TYPES

  TFR (Temporary Flight Restrictions)
    Red overlay. Sourced from FAA data. Warns pilots about restricted
    airspace. Enabled by default.

  Speed Cameras
    Orange overlay. Shows locations of speed cameras and red-light cameras.
    200-meter alert radius. Disabled by default.

  School Zones
    Amber overlay. Shows school locations with 300-meter alert zones.
    Time-filtered: alerts only active on weekdays 7-9 AM and 2-4 PM.
    Disabled by default.

  Flood Zones
    Blue overlay. FEMA National Flood Hazard Layer data. Darker blue for
    high-risk A/V flood zones. Disabled by default.

  Railroad Crossings
    Dark overlay with yellow outline. 100-meter alert radius around
    at-grade railroad crossings. Disabled by default.

ENABLING ZONE OVERLAYS
  In the Alerts menu, toggle each zone type on or off. You can also:
  - Toggle alert sounds on/off.
  - Adjust the alert distance.

HOW ALERTS WORK
  - Entry alerts trigger when you enter a zone (CRITICAL for TFRs,
    WARNING for others).
  - Proximity alerts trigger within 5 nautical miles when heading toward
    a zone (within 60 degrees of bearing).
  - Exit alerts trigger when you leave a zone (informational).
  - Cooldowns prevent repeated alerts: 5 minutes for proximity, 10 minutes
    for entry alerts.
  - Alerts appear in the status line with a color-coded background matching
    the zone type.

GPS AND AIRCRAFT
  - Your GPS position is checked against all enabled geofence zones.
  - When following an aircraft, its position (including altitude) is also
    checked against zones.

14. ZONE DATABASES
================================================================================

In addition to live zone data, you can download pre-built geofence databases
for offline use and expanded coverage.

ACCESSING THE DATABASE MANAGER
  Alerts menu (toolbar bell icon) -> "Zone Databases".

AVAILABLE DATABASES (4 total, 220,000+ zones)

  Military Bases (1,944 zones)
    US military installation polygon boundaries.

  Speed/Red-Light Cameras (109,500 zones)
    Worldwide camera locations with 200-meter alert radius.

  US K-12 Schools (101,390 zones)
    Public school locations with 300-meter alert zones.

  DJI No-Fly Zones (7,823 zones)
    Drone no-fly zones with variable radius.

DOWNLOADING
  In the Database Manager, each database shows its name, zone count, and
  file size. Tap "Download" to download and install. A progress indicator
  shows download status. Once installed, zones from the database are loaded
  into the geofence engine and checked alongside live data.

DELETING
  Tap "Delete" on any installed database to remove it and free storage.

IMPORTING CUSTOM DATABASES
  At the top of the Database Manager are two import buttons:

  IMPORT .DB
    Import a pre-built SQLite geofence database file (.db format).
    The file must contain the correct schema (db_meta and zones tables).
    The app validates the schema before importing.

  IMPORT CSV
    Import a CSV file of zone locations. A configuration dialog lets you:
    - Set a name for the database.
    - Choose the zone type.
    - Set a default radius for point-based zones.
    The CSV is parsed and converted to SQLite format automatically.

EXPORTING
  Tap "Export" on any installed database to share it via Android's share
  system (email, messaging, file manager, etc.).

DUPLICATE HANDLING
  If you import a database with the same ID as an existing one, the app
  asks whether you want to overwrite it.

15. DARK MODE
================================================================================

Tap the moon icon in the toolbar (between the weather icon and alerts icon)
to toggle dark mode.

  Light mode: Standard OpenStreetMap (MAPNIK) tiles.
  Dark mode:  CartoDB Dark Matter tiles with a dark color scheme.

Your preference is saved and persists across app restarts.

16. GO TO LOCATION
================================================================================

Open the grid menu and tap GO TO to navigate to a specific address.

HOW TO USE
  1. Type an address, place name, or landmark in the search field.
  2. Results appear as you type (autocomplete with a 500ms debounce).
  3. Tap a result to navigate the map to that location.
  4. The app automatically searches for POIs at the new location.

Notes:
  - Uses the Photon geocoder (US locations only).
  - Results include address, city, and state for disambiguation.

17. POPULATE POIs
================================================================================

The Populate feature systematically searches for POIs across a wide area,
building up the local database. Access it from the grid menu: Utility ->
Populate POIs.

MANUAL POPULATE
  Starts a grid scanner that spirals outward from the map center:
  - Begins with a 10km probe to assess POI density.
  - Calibrates the search radius based on results.
  - Spirals outward in a grid pattern, searching each cell.
  - Progress appears in the status line.
  - Tap the status banner to stop at any time.

10KM PROBE POPULATE
  An expanding spiral of 10km wide probes for discovering POIs in
  low-density areas:
  - Crosshairs show the probe location (map pans but does not zoom).
  - 30-second countdown between probes.
  - Status line shows the last probe count and recommended fill radius.

IDLE AUTO-POPULATE
  When your GPS position is stationary for 10 minutes, the app automatically
  begins populating POIs around your location:
  - Uses longer delays (45 seconds) between searches to be gentle on APIs.
  - Any interaction (map tap, opening a menu, tapping a button) stops the
    auto-populate and resets the 10-minute idle timer.
  - If stopped, it resumes from where it left off (not from scratch).
  - State is cleared when you long-press, move more than 100 meters,
    use Go To Location, or start a manual populate.

SILENT FILL
  On app startup, restore, or long-press, a single background POI search
  runs automatically after a short delay to quietly fill in the immediate
  area.

18. SOCIAL FEATURES
================================================================================

The social layer lets you register an account, comment on POIs, rate places,
and chat with other users in real time.

--------
REGISTRATION
--------
  Open the grid menu and tap SOCIAL.
  - If you have not registered, a Register dialog appears.
  - Enter a Display Name (2-50 characters), Email, and Password.
  - Tap Register. Your account is created and bonded to your device.
  - You only need to register once. There is no login/logout — your
    device stays authenticated automatically.

  Your profile shows in the PROFILE button (grid menu): avatar initial,
  display name, role, and user ID.

--------
POI COMMENTS
--------
  When you open a POI Detail Dialog (by tapping a marker or via Find),
  a Comments section appears below the POI information.

  VIEWING COMMENTS
    Existing comments show the author name, relative time (e.g., "5m ago"),
    star rating (if given), and the comment text.

  ADDING A COMMENT
    Tap "+ Add" next to the "Comments" header.
    - Select a star rating (1-5, optional).
    - Write your comment (up to 1,000 characters).
    - A character counter ("0 / 1000") shows how many characters you
      have used.
    - Tap "Post" to submit.

  VOTING
    Each comment has upvote and downvote arrows.
    - Tap the up arrow to upvote (turns green with count).
    - Tap the down arrow to downvote.

  DELETING
    You can delete your own comments. Deleted comments show as "[deleted]"
    but preserve the author name and rating.

--------
CHAT
--------
  Open the grid menu and tap CHAT to open the Room List.

  ROOM LIST
    Shows all available chat rooms with name, member count, and description.
    A "Global" room is always available for all users.

  JOINING A ROOM
    Tap any room to enter it. Messages load from history and new messages
    appear in real time.

  SENDING MESSAGES
    Type in the message bar at the bottom and tap send. Your messages
    appear as blue bubbles on the right. Other users' messages appear as
    gray bubbles on the left with their name above.

  CREATING A ROOM
    Tap the "+ New" button in the Room List.
    - Enter a room name (required, up to 100 characters).
    - Enter an optional description (up to 255 characters).
    - Tap Create.

  NAVIGATING ROOMS
    Use the back arrow at the top of a chat room to return to the Room List.
    Each room preserves its message history.

20. TIPS & TRICKS
================================================================================

  - Long-press anywhere on the map to quickly explore that area. The app
    will search for POIs and fetch weather for the pressed location.

  - Use Find + text search to quickly locate a specific place by name
    instead of scrolling through categories.

  - Add frequently visited places to Favorites for one-tap access later.

  - Enable aircraft tracking and tap an airplane to watch its flight path
    draw in real time. The trail is color-coded by altitude.

  - Turn on Radar before a storm to watch precipitation movement with the
    animated 35-minute radar loop.

  - Download the Zone Databases (Military Bases, Speed Cameras, Schools,
    No-Fly Zones) for comprehensive offline geofence coverage with over
    220,000 zones.

  - In dark mode, the map uses CartoDB Dark Matter tiles which are easier
    on the eyes at night and make colored markers stand out more.

  - The idle auto-populate feature is great for building up POI coverage
    while you are stationary. Just leave the app open and it will
    automatically search your surroundings after 10 minutes of inactivity.

  - Share a POI with friends using the Share button in the detail dialog.
    It sends the name, address, phone, hours, and a Google Maps link.

  - Use Go To Location to jump directly to an address without scrolling.
    POIs at the destination load automatically.

  - Filter mode (long-press a category in Find) is useful when you only
    want to see one type of POI on the map, such as just restaurants or
    just gas stations.
