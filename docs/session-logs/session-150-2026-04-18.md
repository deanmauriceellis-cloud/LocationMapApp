## "Why so little historical narration?" — root-cause analysis

Operator's second question. This is the biggest bug of the session.

### The numbers
- 78 total `NarrationState → Speaking` events in the log.
- **All 78 are `type=HINT`. Zero are `type=NARRATION` / `LONG_NARRATION`.**
- Only 6 `NARR-PLAY: text=...` lines appeared — i.e. only a handful of POIs even logged their body text (the rest show `DIRECT PLAY` followed by HINT-only Speaking events).

### What operator heard at each POI
Two short utterances per POI:
1. `"You are at ${point.name}."` — hardcoded orientation line.
2. `shortNarration` — often just a directional hint.

Examples from the walk:
| POI | shortNarration (what played) | long_narration (what didn't) |
|---|---|---|
| Samantha Statue | 45 ch "The Bewitched statue is ahead on Essex Street" | 423 ch |
| Nathaniel Bowditch House | 49 ch "The Nathaniel Bowditch House is at 9 North Street" | 1056 ch |
| John Ward House | 156 ch | 1318 ch |
| The Witch House | 171 ch | 1119 ch |
| Phillips Library | 197 ch | 1029 ch |
| Derby House | 70 ch | 983 ch |

Operator received ~5-20% of the authored historical content at each stop.

### Why

**Bug A: `getNarrationForPass()` ignores AudioControl detail level.**
`NarrationGeofenceManager.kt:297-303` returns `shortNarration` unconditionally (unless `historicalMode` is on, which it wasn't). The Deep detail setting is never consulted on the ambient walk path.

`pickVariantText()` exists in `NarrationManager.kt:159-163` and DOES respect BRIEF/STANDARD/DEEP — but it only works for `TourPoi` (tour-mode). Ambient `SalemPoi` narration bypasses it entirely.

**Bug B: `speakTaggedNarration` is misnamed.**
`TourViewModel.speakTaggedNarration(...)` at line 265-267 just delegates to `narrationManager.speakTaggedHint(...)`. Every utterance — including what was meant to be the body — gets tagged `SegmentType.HINT`. The S145 history-panel kind-inference, the nav cluster Prev/Next routing, and the replay classification all see HINT for everything.

**Bug C: Many shortNarration fields are literal direction-hints.**
49 chars of "X is at Y Street" is not historical content. Even if Bug A were fixed and detailLevel=STANDARD returned shortNarration as intended, half the POIs encountered wouldn't produce any actual history. The content in long_narration / historical_note is what the operator wanted to hear.

**Bug D (the Chestnut-St specific case):** Historic New England's Phillips House was muted by AudioControl (ENTERTAINMENT category) and even if unmuted only has 250 chars of long_narration and zero historical_note. Double fail.

### Shortest path to fix
1. **Task #16:** Make `getNarrationForPass()` detail-aware — BRIEF=name, STANDARD=short, DEEP=long fallback short fallback name. One-file change in `NarrationGeofenceManager.kt`.
2. **Task #17:** Split `speakTaggedNarration` from `speakTaggedHint` so the second segment in the DIRECT PLAY path is properly tagged as `SegmentType.LONG_NARRATION`. Touches `TourViewModel.kt` + `NarrationManager.kt` + the narration-segment kind-inference in the history panel.
3. **Task #9:** Fix the mis-categorized historic houses (Phillips House, Hale Farm, etc.) so they aren't muted.

Without (1), even long-press Audio → DEEP does nothing on a free walk. That's the single change that flips this from "I barely heard any history" to "this feels like a tour."

## Field-test bug #4 — GPS trail not following live GPS (operator mid-session report)

Operator: "I don't see my current gps trail highlighted on the map as I move, just an old gps coords."

**Root cause:** `SalemMainActivity.kt:2817-2862` — the stationary-freeze gate blocks the journey polyline append on every fix where (a) MotionTracker says stationary, and (b) new fix is within 25m of last accepted. The accelerometer-based significant-motion sensor rarely fires in a moving car (phone steady on a mount/seat), so MotionTracker stays stationary the entire drive. The 25m escape hatch means the trail only gets a new point every ~25m — choppy, lagging dots instead of a continuous line. At walking speeds, even worse: 25m takes ~20-30s to cover.

**Fix shipped:** speed-based escape hatch. If `update.speedMps > 0.5` (~1 mph), unfreeze for the entire visible pipeline regardless of motion sensor state. Single-point change in the `stationaryFrozen` run-block. Now the trail updates on every fix during any real motion.

## Field-test bug #5 — GPS polling pinned to 2.5s (log spam + battery)

**Root cause:** `SalemMainActivity.kt:2897` — `val narrationActive = true` hardcoded. The comment described a 3-state ladder (10s driving / 2.5s walking / 60s idle) but the code was only 2-state (10s driving / 2.5s everything else). 494 GPS fixes in a 90-min session, most while parked.
