Suspected my `src/debug/AndroidManifest.xml` was leaking; stashed S249 changes and rebuilt pre-S249 release — same leak present. So **pre-existing**, not S249's fault.

Investigation via `app-salem/build/intermediates/manifest_merge_blame_file/release/.../manifest-merger-blame-release-report.txt` traced the leak to `:core` module's own `core/src/main/AndroidManifest.xml:17-18` — it declared INTERNET + ACCESS_NETWORK_STATE at the library level, which then merged into both debug AND release APKs of every consuming app. The S180 strip from `app-salem/src/main/AndroidManifest.xml` was being silently negated. **Fixed** by removing those two `<uses-permission>` lines from `core/src/main/AndroidManifest.xml` and replacing with a comment block explaining the S180/S249 rationale. Now consuming apps that need network in debug declare INTERNET in their own `src/debug/AndroidManifest.xml` (which app-salem now does, S249).

Effectively in retail: every gated method just early-returns; the OkHttp interceptor always throws; INTERNET permission isn't even declared. Identical V1 ship-gate posture to pre-S249, AND tighter than pre-S249 (the core-manifest leak is now closed).
