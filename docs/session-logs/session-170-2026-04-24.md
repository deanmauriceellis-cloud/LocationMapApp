## Walking-cycle attempts — all failed

Operator: "I don't see the cats walking, they are just rotating … we need animated steps in sync with different angles." Tried three approaches:

### 1. AI 2-pose generation with IPAdapter
Wrote `bake-character.py` that, per character: (a) generates 2 walk-pose images via SD+IPAdapter at weight 0.5, (b) TripoSR each to a mesh, (c) headless renders 16 angles each via new `render-angles.py` (pyrender + EGL, in-process), (d) packs to one multi-pose animated WebP.

Headless renderer **works great** — pyrender + EGL renders 16 angles in seconds, no browser. This unlocks unattended batches.

But the cat output had no visible walking. IPAdapter at 0.5 weight let SD wash out the black (cat went grey) AND the two poses didn't differ enough to read as walking when alternated. Identity drift vs. pose differentiation are in tension; lowering weight further only worsens identity drift.

### 2. Procedural mesh deformation in Blender
Installed Blender 4.2.5 LTS via tarball at `~/AI-Studio/blender/` (no sudo). Headless EGL works, CUDA + OPTIX both detected on the 3090. Wrote `~/AI-Studio/3d-pipeline/blender-walk-render.py` that loads a GLB, splits vertices by Z height, applies sine-wave displacement to lower-body vertices to fake leg lift + body bob + tilt, renders 8 walk × 16 angles = 128 frames in seconds via EEVEE Next.

Result: **mesh crumpled, witch unrecognizable, vertex colors not loaded** (white grayscale only). TripoSR meshes don't have leg/body topological separation; vertex displacement on a continuous blob doesn't produce walking, it produces deformation. Confirmed: procedural walking on AI-generated meshes is not viable.

### 3. Real skeletal rigging — deferred
Honest options remaining:
- **Mixamo** (best for humanoids, free, browser-only — operator must do ~10 min/character of clicks; not automatable from CLI)
- **Quaternius CC0 stock rigged characters** (fully automatable, but loses AI character identity — witch becomes a generic Quaternius woman)
- **Hybrid** — texture stock rigs with AI illustration as billboards or projected textures
- **Auto-Rig Pro** ($45 paid Blender addon)

Operator chose to **leave it as is** for V1 and close the session. Walking is V2 scope.

### Clipping fix shipped
Edge-pixel analysis showed all 7 baked characters were clipping (7-26% edge alpha — body parts off canvas). Scale factor in `render-angles.py` lowered from 1.6/maxDim → 1.1/maxDim. Re-baked all 7 via headless renderer; clipping now ≤1.6% on every character.

### Cross-fade blur fix shipped
Operator noted "blurry two image overlap" between rotation frames. Switched `drawBlendedFrame` from cross-fade between adjacent angle frames to snap-to-nearest. Looks crisper.
