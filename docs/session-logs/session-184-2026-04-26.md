### Iterations on the red highlight

The first version of the red highlight wasn't visible at all on the rendered map. Two stacking issues:

- Within `TourLegsLayer`'s own LayerGroup, the selected leg drew in source order — a leg with a higher `leg_order` than the selected one would draw on top. Fix: pull the selected leg out of the loop and draw it last.
- Even after that, the selected leg was buried under `TourStopLayer`'s blue dashed inter-stop polyline, which renders into `overlayPane` (z 400) AFTER `TourLegsLayer` because of JSX ordering. Fix: created a custom Leaflet pane `tour-selected-leg-pane` at z-index 450 (between `overlayPane` and the colliding `shadowPane` at 500, below `markerPane` at 600) and put the selected leg's two polylines into it. Pane creation moved into the same `useEffect` that draws the polylines (rather than a separate effect) so the pane is guaranteed to exist before any polyline references it.

Operator confirmed the red now reads clearly above the green and the dashed connector, behind the numbered waypoint markers. ✓

### Routing-graph observations (read-only — not engineering work, content rule applies)

While debugging the visual issues, surfaced two structural facts about `tour_WD1`:

- **Legs 10 and 12 routed huge detours.** Leg 10 (waypoints 10 → 11): waypoints 80 m apart, routed 2082 m / 103 points. Leg 12 (waypoints 12 → 13): waypoints 108 m apart, routed 1850 m / 93 points. Stops 10–13 sit on the east edge / interior of Salem Common, where the on-device router's pedestrian graph apparently lacks edges crossing the Common. Per `feedback_tour_routing_is_content_not_engineering.md`, the fix is for the operator to drag those waypoints onto streets the router knows. Operator accepted this and reported "all the waypoints should be on streets now, seems better."

- **Leg 4 → 5 visible "corner-and-back" overshoot.** After the waypoint adjustments, leg 4's routed polyline ended at 42.52038 (~16 m south of waypoint 5's marker at 42.52052), and my anchor code's appended to-marker pulled it back north — visually a dogleg. Generalized fix landed in `TourLegsLayer.drawLeg`: before anchoring, find the polyline-point indices closest to the from/to marker coords and slice the routed geometry to that range. Overshoots get trimmed; undershoots still get the connector. Falls back to the full routed geometry for degenerate cases (`startIdx > endIdx`). Operator confirmed the dogleg gone.
