## Unified Hash Space — Load-Bearing Disclosure (OMEN-008 §5)

All three streams share **the same hash function and salt**. Same physical device produces the same hash in every stream. **Any device that has ever been observed by WWWatcher (which holds raw records by exemption) is identifiable in Salem on contact, with no escalation required.** Salem's hashed-default ceiling protects only devices that have never been seen by WWWatcher.

OMEN-008 requires this be disclosed honestly in Salem's Privacy Policy rather than papered over. **This is the most legally load-bearing element of the disclosure.**

## Three Drafting Constraints for the Privacy Policy

**(a)** List the four feeds individually — GPS, BLE, WiFi probe requests, observable cell-tower information. Match the disclosed scope to the actual `SensorMessage` variants. "Device environment data" is the user-facing blurb wording, not the legal substance.

**(b)** Disclose the unified-hash-space limitation explicitly. Drafting around it would put us out of compliance with OMEN-008 the moment OMEN reviews. Honest disclosure is the legal posture, not a weakness.

**(c)** "Cross-reference with operator security systems" = WWWatcher specifically. The Privacy Policy doesn't have to name WWWatcher, but it must disclose: (i) an observation may trigger an immediate alert to a separate operator security system, (ii) matched records flow into that system's permanent store via the promotion pipeline (§7), (iii) retention there is indefinite (vs Salem 30d raw / 90d hash table rolling).
