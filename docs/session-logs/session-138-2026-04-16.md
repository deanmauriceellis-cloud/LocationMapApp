### Answer to operator on age-gate question (#11 follow-up)

Short answer: **offline-only resolves privacy/data concerns but does NOT automatically resolve age-assurance concerns.** Google Play's age-assurance requirements are tied to **IARC content rating**, not network posture or paid/free status.

- **What offline-only solves:** Data Safety form becomes nearly all "none," COPPA concerns largely dissolve (no data collection = no covered collection of children's data), Privacy Policy gets simpler, no server/compliance ongoing costs. These are real and significant wins.
- **What offline-only does NOT solve:** IARC content rating is driven by the content itself. Salem Witch Trials material (executions, Giles Corey pressing, witchcraft themes, gallows, demonic-possession themes) typically rates **Teen (13+) or higher**, not Everyone. A Teen+ rating can trigger Play's age-assurance flow regardless of whether the app is paid, offline, or privacy-clean.
- **What paid ($19.99) does NOT solve:** There is no documented path where "paying for the app" exempts it from content-rating-driven age assurance. Paid apps with Teen+ ratings still face the same age-gate requirements as free apps with the same rating.
- **Realistic posture:** Let IARC rate the app at submission (probably Teen), accept the resulting age-assurance flow, and don't plan the business model around avoiding it. The offline + deletable-data posture is still a huge policy-compliance win even if it doesn't remove the age gate.
- **Operator next step:** Raise the question with legal aid during the #10 walkthrough — get a definitive read on whether the paid-offline combination changes anything under Google's current Developer Program Policies (last major revision late 2025).

Memory updated to reflect: V1 ships offline-only + $19.99 flat + accept likely Teen rating. Age-assurance exemption is NOT a load-bearing assumption.
