# B1. Tier-2 Holdback Manifest

**What is this?** A list of the documents the operator has prepared but is **not** disclosing in Tier 1. These will be delivered on execution of a mutual NDA or an engagement letter with explicit confidentiality provisions (see [§3 of the cover](#nda-request)).

**Why are they gated?** The operator's primary concern is patent-novelty preservation. A subset of the items below describe candidate inventions whose novelty case would be weakened (or, in the worst case, published) by disclosure to a non-confidentiality-bound counterparty. A second concern is competitive sensitivity of the commercial strategy.

<a name="b1-4-counsel-note"></a>

## 4. A note to counsel

The operator is aware that ABA Model Rule 1.18 extends a confidentiality duty to prospective-client disclosures, and that in many jurisdictions this duty would cover even an initial consultation call. The operator's preference for an **express written** confidentiality agreement reflects three concerns:

1. **Patent-specific stakes.** Unlike most confidentiality concerns, patent novelty is a one-shot resource. A disclosure that is later adjudged to be "public" cannot be rolled back. The cost of an extra signature is low; the cost of misjudging Rule 1.18's scope is catastrophic.
2. **Scope clarity.** The operator is disclosing material that is **not** strictly "legal advice" territory — it includes commercial strategy and technical architecture. An express NDA makes clear the counterparty owes confidentiality across all of it, not just the legal-consultation subset.
3. **Documentation.** If an investor or acquirer later asks about IP hygiene, an executed NDA trail is cleaner than an implied ethics-rule obligation.

An engagement letter with explicit confidentiality provisions would accomplish all three goals and is the operator's preferred path once it is clear counsel is being engaged. A standalone mutual NDA (see [§B2](#b2-mutual-nda-template)) is the fallback for the initial consultation.
