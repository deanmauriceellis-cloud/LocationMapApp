# B2. Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement (Draft Template)

> **Very important disclaimer.** This is an **operator-drafted starting template**, not a counsel-approved document. The operator is not an attorney and this document has not been reviewed by one. Counsel is welcome — and encouraged — to substitute their own standard mutual-NDA template. This draft is provided solely to unblock the Monday meeting if counsel does not have a template immediately available. **It should not be signed without counsel review.**

---

<a name="b2-1-form"></a>

## Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement

This Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into on **[EFFECTIVE DATE]** between:

- **Dean Maurice Ellis**, an individual residing in [Massachusetts], acting in his personal capacity and on behalf of a to-be-formed corporation which shall be a successor-in-interest to the rights and obligations hereunder upon its formation ("**Party A**"); and
- **[COUNSEL NAME / LAW FIRM NAME]**, with an address at [COUNSEL ADDRESS] ("**Party B**").

Each of Party A and Party B is a "**Party**," and together the "**Parties**."

### 1. Purpose

The Parties wish to explore a potential engagement for legal services in connection with Party A's mobile application *Katrina's Mystic Visitors Guide* and its attendant incorporation, intellectual property, and commercial-release matters (the "**Purpose**"). In connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose to the other certain confidential and proprietary information.

### 2. Confidential Information

**"Confidential Information"** means any information disclosed by one Party (the "**Disclosing Party**") to the other (the "**Receiving Party**"), whether orally, visually, in writing, or by any other means, that is:

- marked or identified as "confidential," "proprietary," or similar at the time of disclosure; or
- reasonably understood, under the circumstances, to be confidential or proprietary.

Confidential Information includes, without limitation: (a) technical information, inventions, algorithms, source code, architecture, and know-how; (b) product plans, feature roadmaps, and release schedules; (c) commercial information, including pricing, tier strategy, market analysis, and financial projections; (d) intellectual property inventories, patent novelty analyses, and claim sketches; (e) customer, supplier, and vendor information; and (f) any compilation, analysis, or derivative work that includes any of the foregoing.

### 3. Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by written records:

- was already in its rightful possession, without confidentiality obligation, before disclosure by the Disclosing Party;
- is or becomes publicly known through no act or omission of the Receiving Party;
- is rightfully received from a third party without confidentiality obligation; or
- is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information.

### 4. Obligations

The Receiving Party shall:

- use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose;
- protect the Confidential Information with the same degree of care it uses to protect its own confidential information of like importance, and no less than a reasonable degree of care;
- not disclose the Confidential Information to any third party except (i) its employees, contractors, or professional advisors who have a need to know for the Purpose and who are bound by written confidentiality obligations at least as protective as those in this Agreement, or (ii) as required by §6 (Compelled Disclosure);
- not copy, modify, or reverse-engineer any Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Purpose; and
- not use any Confidential Information to develop a product or service that competes with that of the Disclosing Party.

### 5. Patent-Novelty Protections

The Parties acknowledge that certain Confidential Information disclosed by Party A may include material that is the subject of pending or contemplated patent applications. The Receiving Party agrees that it will not:

- publish, file, or cause to be filed any patent application that claims or incorporates any Confidential Information of the Disclosing Party;
- use any Confidential Information in a manner that would constitute "public disclosure" or "public use" as those terms are understood under 35 U.S.C. § 102; or
- disclose or reference any Confidential Information in any publication, conference presentation, or other public venue.

Nothing in this section grants the Disclosing Party a right to bar the Receiving Party from continuing its own, independent line of development that does not incorporate Confidential Information.

### 6. Compelled Disclosure

If the Receiving Party is required by law, subpoena, court order, or regulatory demand to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall (to the extent legally permissible) promptly notify the Disclosing Party so the Disclosing Party may seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy, and the Receiving Party shall cooperate with any such effort at the Disclosing Party's expense.

### 7. No License

No license under any patent, copyright, trademark, or other intellectual property right is granted or implied by this Agreement or by any disclosure hereunder. Each Party retains all right, title, and interest in and to its own Confidential Information.

### 8. Return or Destruction

Upon written request of the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall (at its election) promptly return or destroy all tangible materials containing Confidential Information and, on request, certify such return or destruction in writing, except for one archival copy retained by counsel for record-keeping purposes, which remains subject to this Agreement.

### 9. Term and Survival

This Agreement commences on the Effective Date and continues for **five (5) years** thereafter, unless earlier terminated by mutual written agreement. The confidentiality obligations in §4 and §5, and the patent-novelty protections in §5, shall survive termination of this Agreement for so long as the applicable Confidential Information retains its confidential character. Notwithstanding the foregoing, obligations in respect of trade secrets shall survive for so long as the information retains trade-secret status under applicable law.

### 10. No Obligation

This Agreement does not obligate either Party to (a) enter into any further business or professional relationship, (b) disclose any particular Confidential Information, or (c) refrain from negotiating or entering into agreements with third parties for similar purposes.

### 11. Remedies

The Parties agree that monetary damages may be inadequate for a breach of this Agreement and that the Disclosing Party shall be entitled, in addition to all other remedies, to seek equitable relief, including injunction and specific performance, without the need to post a bond.

### 12. Attorney-Client Privilege (Party B as Law Firm)

The Parties acknowledge that Party B is being consulted in the capacity of prospective legal counsel. Nothing in this Agreement shall limit or waive the attorney-client privilege, work-product doctrine, or any ethical obligation of confidentiality that Party B owes to Party A under applicable rules of professional conduct (including, without limitation, ABA Model Rule 1.6 and Rule 1.18). To the extent any conflict exists between this Agreement and such professional obligations, the obligation providing greater protection to Party A's Confidential Information shall govern.

### 13. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the **[STATE TBD]**, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. Any disputes arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in **[COUNTY AND STATE TBD]**, and each Party consents to personal jurisdiction there.

### 14. Entire Agreement; Amendments

This Agreement is the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter and supersedes any prior oral or written understanding. It may be amended only by a writing signed by both Parties.

### 15. Counterparts and Electronic Signatures

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which is an original, and may be signed electronically. Electronic or scanned signatures shall be deemed originals.

### 16. Successors and Assigns

This Agreement binds and benefits the Parties and their respective successors, assigns, and legal representatives. Party A's to-be-formed corporation (see preamble) shall be deemed a successor-in-interest and shall assume the rights and obligations of Party A hereunder upon its formation, without the need for further writing.

## Operator drafting notes

1. **Term length.** Five years is the operator's default; counsel may prefer shorter (3 years) or specific-to-trade-secret perpetual. Counsel's call.
2. **Patent-novelty clause (§5) is deliberately explicit.** Standard mutual NDAs rarely spell out the 35 U.S.C. § 102 implications; this draft does because the operator's primary concern is patent preservation. Counsel may want to soften or strengthen.
3. **Successors clause (§16) pre-binds the to-be-formed C-corp.** This avoids a re-sign step after incorporation. Counsel should confirm enforceability under the operator's eventual state of incorporation.
4. **Governing law and forum (§13) is left [TBD].** Counsel will decide based on the operating-entity state.
5. **ABA Rule 1.18 cross-reference (§12) is explicit.** This is unusual in an NDA but the operator wants it clear that the NDA layers on top of, and does not replace, counsel's ethical confidentiality duties.
6. **No residual-knowledge clause.** Some NDAs include a "residuals" carve-out permitting use of retained mental impressions. The operator's draft omits it because it would weaken patent-novelty protections.
7. **Counsel's preferred template is welcome as a substitute.** The operator's only non-negotiables are:
   (a) the patent-novelty protections of §5 (or equivalent language),
   (b) the successors clause of §16 (so the C-corp inherits the protection),
   (c) an express term of at least three years.
