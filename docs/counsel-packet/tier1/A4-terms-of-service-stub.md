**Status:** DRAFT — non-attorney draft produced by the operator as a starting point. Counsel is expected to replace or heavily revise this with a standard mobile-app ToS template tuned to the V1 posture.
**Scope:** Minimum-viable posture that closes the "no ToS on the store listing" gap without introducing anything counsel will have to walk back.
**Hosting target:** `destructiveaigurus.com/katrinas-mystic-guide/terms` (HTTPS, public).
**Drafted:** 2026-04-19 (operator Session 151).

<a name="a4-1-scope-note"></a>

## 1. Scope note — what this stub is and is not

This stub is written for the V1 posture:

- **Paid** ($19.99 flat one-time purchase through Google Play)
- **Fully offline** (no servers, no accounts, no network I/O beyond bundled assets)
- **No user-generated content**
- **No ads, no subscriptions, no in-app purchases**
- **One developer, one jurisdiction**

Because V1 is this small, the terms can be this short. V2 (if it introduces server-side features, user-generated content, or subscriptions) will need a proper full Terms of Service.

**What is NOT in this stub (and counsel should decide whether to add):**

- Arbitration clause and class-action waiver
- Choice-of-law-and-venue clause (beyond naming the operating entity's state)
- Indemnification clause (operator → user, and/or user → operator)
- Export-control representation (EAR / OFAC)
- DMCA takedown procedure (may not be required since there is no UGC)
- Accessibility statement
- Data-processing addendum (N/A — no data processing happens)

## 3. Counsel review checklist

- [ ] Replace this stub with counsel's standard mobile-app ToS template, OR approve this stub for V1 with minor edits.
- [ ] Decide whether to include an **arbitration clause + class-action waiver** (industry standard for US paid apps; protective for the operator; may raise consumer-friendliness concerns).
- [ ] Decide whether to add a **DMCA takedown procedure** (V1 has no user-generated content, so may not be required).
- [ ] Decide on **choice of forum** beyond just governing law — specific county/state courts, or bind to JAMS/AAA arbitration.
- [ ] Confirm the **"as-is" / no-warranty disclaimer** (§2.3) is strong enough for the operator's risk tolerance.
- [ ] Confirm the **limitation of liability cap** (§2.9) at "amount paid for the app" — some jurisdictions require a higher floor; counsel should flag.
- [ ] Confirm the **governing-law clause** (§2.11) ties to the C-corp's state of incorporation, not the operator's personal state.
- [ ] Confirm that the ToS and Privacy Policy **cross-reference consistently** (§2.12 here points to Privacy Policy §2.10; make sure both exist and are compatible).
- [ ] Decide on **ToS versioning** — should we publish a version number as well as a date, so users can reference specific versions later?
- [ ] Decide **where to host** — same pattern as Privacy Policy: `destructiveaigurus.com/katrinas-mystic-guide/terms`.
- [ ] Decide whether to **link the ToS inside the app** (Settings → About → Terms) in addition to the store listing.
